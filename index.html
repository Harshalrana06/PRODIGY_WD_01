Indian Subcontinent

Explore the geography, culture, and history of South Asia

Geography

The Indian Subcontinent is a large, distinct landmass in South Asia, separated from the rest of Asia by the Himalayas. It includes diverse geographic features such as the Thar Desert, the Indo-Gangetic Plain, the Deccan Plateau, and the Ghats.

Culture

The subcontinent is a cradle of ancient civilizations and religions. It is home to a rich blend of traditions, festivals, languages, cuisines, and spiritual practices.

History

From the Indus Valley Civilization to British colonization, the Indian Subcontinent has a long, storied past. It has seen the rise and fall of empires, the spread of religions, and pivotal freedom movements.

Countries

The Indian Subcontinent includes:

  • India
  • Pakistan
  • Bangladesh
  • Nepal
  • Bhutan
  • Sri Lanka
  • Maldives
